--- title: "How do populations respond across environmental stress gradients?" excerpt: "Chamaecrista
" collection: portfolio --- Often "stress" responses within and between populations are measured between binary extremes (ex. drought vs. well-watered). However, while extremes are expected to becoming increasingly common in some areas of the globe, natural populations likely experience environmental stressors along a gradient. I'm interested in evaluating potential changes in traits, plasticity, and maternal effects under finer-scale stress gradients to better understand the thresholds and nuances of plant population response and evolutionary trajectories to stressful and novel enviornments.
